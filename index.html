Charlotte Brontë

British Novelist and Poet
"I am no bird; and no net ensnares me: I am a free human being with an independent will."

-Charlotte Brontë, Jane Eyre

Timeline
  • 1816 - Born in Thornton, Yorkshire, England
  • 1824 - The four oldest Brontë sisters were enrolled at boarding school. The two eldest sisters grew ill and died. Charlotte and Emily were brought home.
  • 1826 - Siblings Charlotte, Emily, Branwell, and Ann created and wrote many books about an imaginary world called Angria.
  • 1835 - After completing her schooling, Charlotte became a governess at Roe Head School for three years. She then held two governess positions for brief periods of time.
  • 1842 - In order to start their own school, Charlotte and Emily went to Brussels to complete their studies.
  • 1846 - The three Brontë sisters were unable to open a school as there was no response from the public. They focused on writing and published a selection of their poems under the pseudonyms Currer, Ellis, and Acton Bell. Charlotte also completed The Professor, but it was rejected for publication.
  • 1847 - Charlotte's Jane Eyre, Emily's Wuthering Heights, and Ann's Agnes Grey were all published under their pseudonyms.
  • 1848 - Charlotte and Ann visited their publishers in London to reveal their true identities. Their brother Branwell died that same year. Emily died shortly after and Ann died the following year.
  • 1852 - Charlotte published Villette.
  • 1854 - Charlotte married Rev. A.B. Nicholls. Charlotte became pregnant and very ill. She passed away before the end of her pregnancy. The most likely cause of death was dehydration and malnourishment due to vomiting caused by severe morning sickness or hyperemesis gravidarum.
Brontë's Published Novels
  • Jane Eyre, published in 1847
  • Shirley, published in 1849
  • Villette, published in 1853
  • The Professor, published in 1857 (Published after Brontë's death)
